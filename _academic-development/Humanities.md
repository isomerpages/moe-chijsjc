<h4><strong>Our Objectives</strong></h4>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To inspire the passion for Humanities in students and help them understand
the interconnectedness of Singapore and the world and relevance in their
learning.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To equip students with critical thinking skills and 21st century competencies
that will allow them to thrive in an increasingly complex world.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To nurture students who are concerned and active citizens committed to
contribute to the society and world they live in.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Our Approach</strong></h4>
<p><strong><em>1. Key pedagogies: Inquiry-based &amp; Issue-based Learning in the Humanities Curriculum</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Use of questions, problems, and scenarios to spark curiosity among students
and help them learn through their own investigation and reflection. These
approaches help to encourage critical thinking, investigation and problem-solving
in the exploration of questions or issues. Students would be equipped with
21st century skills to become independent and self-directed learners.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Facilitation by teachers to guide students’ thinking and help them make
sense of the wealth of information available to them.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Geographical, Historical and Social Studies Issue Investigations through
field-based and experiential learning offer opportunities for students
to engage in the inquiry process.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>2. Nurturing the passion for Humanities</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Nurture the passion for Humanities through various experiential learning
opportunities such as local and overseas learning journeys, competitions
and enrichment programmes.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Make learning of Humanities meaningful by deepening students’ disciplinary
understanding and engaging students in discussion of real-world issues
associated with the Humanities.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Provide opportunities for interdisciplinary explorations for students
to make connections between Humanities subjects, and with other disciplines
like Art, Science and Math.</p>
</li>
</ul>
