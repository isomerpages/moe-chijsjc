<p>Every SJCian matters and the school aims to nurture a caring culture and positive climate for all.</p><p>Students learn&nbsp;well when there is order and discipline. At the same time, they need care, encouragement and space to&nbsp;learn from mistakes and improve. Discipline is an educative process, and the goal is to instill self-discipline&nbsp;and good character in SJCians, equipping them with the necessary social and emotional skills to behave&nbsp;thoughtfully and be responsible and respectful individuals.</p><p>Every SJCian is expected to be an individual with exemplary character and positive attitude towards school&nbsp;and the community. The following Code of Conduct should guide SJCians’ behaviour and actions at all&nbsp;times.</p><h4><strong>1. MORNING ASSEMBLY</strong></h4><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>SJCians who are Singapore Citizens must sing the National Anthem and take the Pledge.</p></li><li><p>SJCians will take the Pledge with their right fist over their heart.</p></li><li><p>All SJCians should stand at attention and face the flags when the National Anthem is being sung and the National Pledge recited.</p></li></ul><h4><strong>2. ATTENDANCE &amp; PUNCTUALITY</strong></h4><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Daily attendance during term time is compulsory. The same is expected for CCA and any&nbsp;activity organised by the school.</p></li><li><p>SJCians should submit to their Form Teacher, upon return to school, a valid Medical&nbsp;Certificate or Letter of Excuse (not more than 4 per term) from parents or guardians.</p></li><li><p>Absence from school without valid reason is considered as truancy.</p></li><li><p>SJCians should be punctual for school, CCA and other school activities.</p></li><li><p>SJCians should be present for morning assembly by 7.30 a.m.</p></li><li><p>SJCians are not allowed to leave the school premises before their scheduled dismissal time&nbsp;without permission.</p></li><li><p>SJCians who need to leave before the scheduled dismissal time should complete the ‘Early&nbsp;Release Form’. Parents or a close relative must give consent for students to be released early.</p></li></ul><h4><strong>3. GENERAL BEHAVIOUR</strong></h4><p><strong><em>In school:</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>SJCians should show kindness and consideration to others at all times, to promote a positive&nbsp;And safe environment for teaching and learning.</p></li><li><p>SJCians are also expected to be responsible for the safekeeping of their personal belongings and considerate in the use of all school equipment and facilities.</p></li><li><p>Every SJCian is responsible for the locker assigned to her. The lockers are school property&nbsp;and may be searched by school authorities.</p></li><li><p>All food and drinks should only be consumed in the canteen.</p></li><li><p>Electronic devices, including mobile phones and any other communication devices should&nbsp;be switched off and kept away during school hours and CCA time, unless otherwise instructed by the teacher.</p></li><li><p>All Personal Learning Devices (PLDs) used in school must be installed with a device management application (DMA). Devices without DMAs should not be used.</p></li><li><p>The school reserves the right to confiscate any electronic device if they are not used&nbsp;responsibly and appropriately. Once confiscated, the school may require the presence of a&nbsp;parent or guardian before the confiscated device is returned to the student.</p></li><li><p>SJCians should not charge their personal electronic devices in school, including their mobile&nbsp;phones and Personal Learning Devices (PLDs).</p></li><li><p>SJCians are not allowed to have in their possession any weapons. They are also not allowed&nbsp;to bring any weapon-like item which is used or intended to be used to cause harm to others.</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>In the classroom:</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>SJCians should observe the GROW protocol, as every lesson is an opportunity to grow in&nbsp;character and learning:</p></li></ul><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Student%20Development/Code%20of%20Conduct/grow.png"></div><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>SJCians will be good ambassadors of the school and uphold the image of SJC. They should&nbsp; observe rules and regulations within the areas they are patronising or visiting at all times.</p></li><li><p>SJCians should also show respect for public space and the community.</p></li></ul><h4><strong>4. ATTIRE &amp; GROOMING</strong></h4><p><strong><em>School Uniform</em></strong></p><p>SJCians are to wear the prescribed uniform within and outside of school. Modification to the&nbsp;uniform is not allowed.</p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Pinafore must touch the knees</p></li><li><p>Collar should be buttoned</p></li><li><p>Only clean, predominantly white, low cut shoes and school or plain white socks are to be worn</p></li><li><p>Loose or low socks are not allowed (the SJC logo must be seen)</p></li><li><p>On days with Physical Education (PE) lessons, students are allowed to wear the PE T-shirt under their pinafore</p></li><li><p>Only the school PE/House T-shirt and shorts should be worn for PE lessons</p></li><li><p>Track shoes may be worn only during PE lessons</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>Hair</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Short hair styles must not touch the collar of the blouse</p></li><li><p>Very short, shaven or undercut hairstyles are not allowed</p></li><li><p>Long hair must be tied neatly with white, blue or black accessories</p></li><li><p>The fringe should not touch the eyebrows</p></li><li><p>Dyed/highlighted hair should be re-dyed to the original colour</p></li><li><p>Hair extensions are not allowed</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>Nails</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Nails are to be kept short and clean, without nail polish</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>Cosmetics and Make-up</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Cosmetics (e.g. foundation, sunblock with foundation base, powder, lipstick, tinted lip balm, eyeliner, mascara, etc.) are not to be used</p></li><li><p>Eyebrows should not be drawn or embroidered</p></li><li><p>Eyelashes should not be permed or have extensions</p></li><li><p>Coloured or colour-enhancing contact lenses are not allowed</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>Jewellery and Accessories</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Only one pair of plain, simple, identical ear studs can be worn on the ear lobes</p></li><li><p>Jewellery and accessories of any other kind (e.g. tongue studs, nose ring, lip piercings, bracelets, ankle bands, bangles, chains, rings, friendship bands, etc.) are not allowed</p></li><li><p>Inappropriate body art (such as tattoos and the piercing of any part of the body except the&nbsp;ears) is not allowed.</p></li></ul><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" BULLYING</strong></h4><p>In CHIJ Schools, we uphold the basic principle of respecting the dignity of each person we interact with. Everyone in our school community has the responsibility and a role to play in building a safe learning environment where we treat each other with respect and understanding. class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Student Development/Code of Conduct/Stop_bullying.jpg"></div><h4><strong>EXAMINATION RULES</strong></h4><p>The examination rules apply to all forms of formal assessment, i.e. Weighted Assessments (WA), Semestral Assessments (SA) and Preliminary Examinations. Students should read, understand and comply fully with the examination rules. They are also responsible for checking and noting the dates and times of the various assessments.</p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>You must be properly attired in your school uniform.</p></li><li><p>You should be punctual as no additional time for the paper will be allocated for late-comers.</p></li><li><p>Be seated at your assigned desk at least fifteen minutes before the commencement of the examination. (Applicable to SA and Preliminary Examination only)</p></li><li><p>Place your NRIC or other identification documents on your desk for verification during the examination. (Applicable to SA and Preliminary Examination only)</p></li><li><p>You are not permitted to have in your possession, while in the examination venues, any devices or materials not authorised for use in the examination. If these are found in your possession, you will be deemed as having committed an act of dishonesty and may be awarded a zero mark for the paper.</p></li></ul><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Student%20Development/Code%20of%20Conduct/exam.png"></div><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>No form of communication is allowed among candidates in the examination venue.</p></li><li><p>Food should not be consumed in the examination venue.</p></li><li><p>Borrowing of stationery during the examination is not allowed.</p></li><li><p>The use of correction fluid/ tape on your answer script or writing paper is not allowed.</p></li><li><p>You are not allowed to leave the examination venue unescorted or without permission from the invigilator.</p></li><li><p>Answer scripts that are removed from the examination venue will not be accepted for grading by the examiners.</p></li><li><p>Disciplinary action will be taken against any student who breaches the examination rules.</p></li></ul><p><strong><em>Absence during an examination</em></strong></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>If you are unable to sit for any examination due to illness, you must provide a Medical Certificate (MC) to cover your absence. Parent’s letter will not be accepted.</p></li><li><p>Inform the school or your Form Teacher of your absence. There will be no make-up examination for papers missed.</p></li><li><p>The MC, issued by a medical practitioner registered under the Medical Registration Act, must be&nbsp;submitted to your Form Teacher upon your return to school.</p></li><li><p>If you do not have a medical certificate to cover your absence, you may be given a ‘zero’ (0) for the papers missed.</p></li></ul><p></p>
